ARE277 - Transversality and Linear Optimal Control

 

11 April 2012

Transversality Conditions ------------------------- (See Leonard and Long (1992), Chapter 7) Our optimization problem is: $$J\{u\} = \int_o^T g(x_t,t,u_t) dt$$ $$\dot{x} = f(x_t, t, u_t)$$ $$x_0, x_T$$ $x_t$ is our state variable, and $u_t$ is our control function. $g(\cdot)$ is the utility function. Discounting isn't explicit, but is part of our $g(\cdot)$. Transversality conditions allow us to solve for different $x_0$ and $x_T$ conditions. ### Case 1: No explicit time point.
